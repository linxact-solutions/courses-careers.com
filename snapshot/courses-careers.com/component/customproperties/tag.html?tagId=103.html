Fashion and Textiles Undergraduate Degree Courses

The world of fashion is ever changing, over the past twenty years the role of fashion designers has developed considerably, many are multi skilled, not only focusing on the aesthetic, but having the ability to work with and design appropriate materials for their garments. Often, a key element of fashion design can be the fabric print, embroidery or knit, with some garments harnessing all three. Consequently, it is a necessity for designers to have a larger toolkit and a broader understanding of industrial requirements.

Art and Design Foundation Courses

Most students complete a one year art and design foundation course after their A-levels, as a stepping stone from secondary to higher education. Holding an art and design foundation diploma is almost a necessity when looking to study a creative subject at degree level. It demonstrates a willing to learn and a commitment to your chosen field of study, as well as a well rounded portfolio and one years worth of experience of working in a studio based, self motivated environment.

MA (Masters Degree) in Product Design

An MA in Product Design is an intensive one-two year course (depending on whether you opt to study full or part time) that aims to further and hone your skill set and experience in the design industries. If you have a keen interest in everyday products and wish to gain a well-rounded understanding of the design decisions that led to their development, this course is for you.

MA (Masters Degree) in Illustration

If you are a talented, ambitious and self motivated artist who wishes to pursue a career as an illustrator, animator or image maker, then the MA in Illustration is the right course for you. An MA in Illustration is typically a one year, full time course, but many institutions offer the option to study part time over two to three years, so it can be a flexible option that fits around current employment or other academic pursuits.

MA (Masters Degree) in Graphic Design
(Postgraduate)
MA (Masters Degree) in Graphic DesignThe role of graphic designers is constantly developing and expanding as the demand for innovative visual communication increases. An MA in Graphic Design will help you become an influential and skilled practitioner, helping you to enhance your existing design skills and develop your ability to think strategically and create relevant, appropriate work from a brief. An MA in Graphic Design teaches you to address wider design issues, enhance your subject expertise and social and cultural awareness. To be a successful designer, you need to have creative ingenuity, as well as in depth design knowledge and of course the technical skills which allow you to implement these ideas. An MA in Graphic Design will allow you to enhance all of these areas and help facilitate the development of a multi-skilled designer. How is an MA in Graphic Design Structured?The format of an MA in Graphic Design varies between institutions. Many are studio based with an emphasis on research and limited teaching time, but others offer more contact with your tutor. Most have learning outcomes or set modules that must be completed throughout the year, but on the whole an MA requires a great deal of self discipline and focus.An MA in Graphic Design focuses on experimentation and creativity. Often this course is taken by not only recent graduates but professional designers who wish to re-visit more creative projects after a number of years working on more structured commercial ventures. The MA in Graphic Design is a perfect opportunity for students to undertake a project they have had a long standing wish to explore. This can be on almost any topic, as many courses are open to individual interpretation of what Graphic Design is and where it’s boundaries lye. More recently, institutions have steered towards a much more conceptual approach, allowing students the freedom to decide how their ideas develop and how they are presented. Much of the time – as long as you are able to explain your idea, its format and its relevance to your original brief, the possibilities are endless.What are my career options after an MA in Graphic Design? Recent global studies have demonstrated that the growth rate of creative and cultural industries is more that twice that of the worlds entire economy. In addition, almost 50% of the UK’s design industries are based in London.Graphic designers produce design solutions to communicate their clients' messages with maximum visual impact. Graphic design skills are essential across the business world in order to portray and sell ideas, products and activities such as advertising, websites, posters, leaflets, books, magazines, packaging, exhibitions and displays and branding. Working to a brief set by the client, a graphic designer’s role is to develop concepts and visuals that best meet the client’s requirements. In order to be a successful designer, you will need excellent technical knowledge, the ability to think ‘outside the box’ and good organization and time management skills.Typical career opportunities include work as consultant designers, CEOs, in-house designers, freelance designers in; graphics, advertising, corporate communication, website designers, packaging and illustration.If you choose to work for a Graphic Design agency, you would typically start at ‘Junior’ level and dependant on your ability and the size and structure of the company, you will move up to ‘Middleweight’ designer, and further into your career you will be promoted to ‘Heavyweight’ designer and eventually Art Director or Head of Design.What are the entry requirements?Successful applicants to an MA in Graphic Design would usually hold an Honours degree within a design subject and have an up to date design portfolio. Those with relevant professional qualifications and/or extensive professional design experience would also be considered. It is important that you research what your chosen institution is looking for in a portfolio and to an extent tailor it accordingly. If their emphasis is on typography for instance, make sure you have examples of this to show at your interview.
